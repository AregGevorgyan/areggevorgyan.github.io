About

Hello! I am a first-year student at Harvard University studying mathematics and computer science. I am also a competitive programmer and musician.

I love programming and am usually working on side projects, especially those that combine science and art in interesting ways. My favorite languages are Python, C++, and JavaScript, though I have worked with a wide variety of languages and technologies in the past.

My academic interests are broadly in computer science. Right now, I am exploring theoretical computer science, programming languages, and machine learning, although I am still deciding where I will focus in the future. I am currently an undergraduate researcher in the Predictive Medicine Group under Ben Reis, and I will be a teaching fellow for CS 124 (Data Structures and Algorithms) in Spring 2020.

Competitive Programming

In high school, I won two gold medals representing the US at the International Olympiad in Informatics (IOI). I am still actively involved in the competitive programming space.

Programming competitions I have helped organize or written/tested problems for include:

  • PWSH Programming Contest
  • USA Computing Olympiad (USACO)
  • National Olympiad in Informatics Philippines (NOI.PH)
  • HackerEarth CodeStar Contest

I maintain an algorithm and data structure library available here. If you are looking for tutoring, I teach online and summer camp courses with Alphastar Academy for students aiming to perform at a high level in the USACO contests.
